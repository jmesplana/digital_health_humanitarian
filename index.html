Health and Care

The International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies (IFRC) supports people's health throughout their entire lifetime, in emergency and non-emergency settings. Our work spans health promotion, prevention, diagnosis, treatment, rehabilitation and palliative care - now enhanced through innovative digital health solutions.

Empowering our health workforce and volunteers with digital tools and technologies to deliver better care, make informed decisions, and reach more communities effectively and efficiently.

RCRC Health Information System

The Red Cross and Red Crescent Health Information System (RCHIS) is a digital tool designed for efficient patient information management in emergency responses.

  • Enhances patient care by securely tracking treatment at every stage.
  • Supports clinicians with streamlined reporting and collaboration tools.
  • Built-in WHO EMT Minimum Data Set (MDS) Report
  • Offline capability for remote locations

Community-Based Surveillance (CBS) Nyss

CBS Nyss is a cloud-based and open-source platform that supports real-time community-based surveillance for early detection and response to health risks.

  • Real-time data collection, management, and analysis for community-based surveillance (CBS).
  • Uses SMS-based reporting from trained volunteers to detect and report health risks.
  • Sends automated alerts for rapid responses to potential outbreaks.
  • Developed by Norwegian Red Cross, in cooperation with the IFRC and Belgium Red Cross.

Universal First Aid Application

The Universal First Aid App provides users with life-saving first aid guidance and resources, accessible anytime, anywhere.

  • Offers step-by-step first aid instructions for various emergencies.

Emergency Health

The IFRC's Emergency Health work addresses health needs during crises by supporting National Societies in their preparedness, response, and recovery efforts:

  • Responds to the immediate health impacts of disasters and public health emergencies.
  • Builds community capacity for infectious disease prevention, detection, and response.
  • Provides psychosocial and mental health support in crisis situations.
  • Deploys Emergency Response Units (ERUs) to deliver clinical and public health interventions.

Red Channel Agreement

The renewed WHO-IFRC Red Channel Agreement, a Memorandum of Understanding on Emergency Medical Teams (EMTs), strengthens cooperation to improve emergency healthcare response. Key highlights include:

  • Aligns IFRC Emergency Response Units with WHO's global EMT standards.
  • Enhances collaboration and training for emergency response teams.
  • Ensures consistent and high-quality healthcare in crises like the Ukraine war and African cholera outbreaks.
  • Fosters accountability and rapid response in global health emergencies.

Protracted Crises & Complex Emergencies

The report, Learning from Red Cross/Red Crescent Assistance in Protracted Crises and Complex Emergencies, examines ways the IFRC and National Societies can enhance support in complex, long-term crisis environments.

  • Flexibility in resources and strategic planning to adapt to evolving challenges in complex crises.
  • Ensures consistent and high-quality healthcare in crises like the Ukraine war and African cholera outbreaks.

IFRC GO Platform

The IFRC GO platform is a digital tool by the IFRC to support disaster response by providing real-time, accessible information and resources for effective decision-making in emergencies.

  • Connects responders with real-time data from the field, enhancing situational awareness.
  • Offers visual reports, maps, and dashboards to support operational decision-making.
  • Integrates data from various humanitarian sources for a comprehensive emergency overview.
  • Aims to improve response times and coordination across the Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement.
