Health and Care | Digital Health Solutions and Data

The International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies (IFRC) supports people's health throughout their entire lifetime, in emergency and non-emergency settings. Our work spans health promotion, prevention, diagnosis, treatment, rehabilitation and palliative care - now enhanced through innovative digital health solutions.

Empowering our health workforce and volunteers with digital tools and technologies to deliver better care, make informed decisions, and reach more communities effectively and efficiently.

RCRC Health Information System

The Red Cross and Red Crescent Health Information System (RCHIS) is a digital tool designed for efficient patient information management in emergency responses.

  • Enhances patient care by securely tracking treatment at every stage.
  • Supports clinicians with streamlined reporting and collaboration tools.
  • Built-in WHO EMT Minimum Data Set (MDS) Report
  • Offline capability for remote locations

Community-Based Surveillance (CBS) Nyss

CBS Nyss is a cloud-based and open-source platform that supports real-time community-based surveillance for early detection and response to health risks.

  • Real-time data collection, management, and analysis for community-based surveillance (CBS).
  • Uses SMS-based reporting from trained volunteers to detect and report health risks.
  • Sends automated alerts for rapid responses to potential outbreaks.
  • Developed by Norwegian Red Cross, in cooperation with the IFRC and Belgium Red Cross.

Universal First Aid Application

The Universal First Aid App provides users with life-saving first aid guidance and resources, accessible anytime, anywhere.

  • Offers step-by-step first aid instructions for various emergencies.
  • Available offline, ensuring access without internet in critical situations.
  • Multilingual and customizable to suit local contexts and needs.
  • Available on IOS and Android

IFRC Catalogue of Surge Services

The IFRC Surge Catalogue is a resource hub for emergency response assets, providing essential information on tools, teams, and deployment processes for crisis support:

  • Lists deployable resources like Emergency Response Units and specialized teams.
  • Outlines standardized procedures for rapid mobilization.
  • Emphasizes flexibility and adaptability to various crisis scenarios.
  • Supports National Societies in leveraging international resources for effective, timely response.
